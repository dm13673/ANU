Introduction

General data quality checks

  1. Chart of raw population data from input database (IDB) by calendar year, age, and sex
    Confirm range of age intervals across time is correct. link
  2. Chart of raw deaths counts from input database (IDB) by calendar year, age-interval, and sex
    Confirm range of age intervals across time is correct. link
  3. Lexis map of Lexis database (LDB) by calendar year, age, and sex.
    Confirm that data in LDB make sense. link
  4. Chart of outliers in distribution of deaths in age range prior to open age interval (OAI)
    Check that procedure for smoothing deaths before fitting model to split OAI is reasonable. link

Internal consistency checks

  1. Chart of total number of deaths by year for IDB versus LDB by sex
    Total from IDB should match exactly to total in LDB. link
  2. Chart of total January 1st population by year for IDB versus LDB by sex
    Total from IDB should track close to total in LDB. link
  3. Chart of deaths within age groups (under 5, 5 to 64, 65 or higher, 85 or higher) by year for IDB versus LDB
    For each age group specific chart, IDB should track close to LDB. link
  4. Chart of population size within age groups (under 5, 5 to 64, 65 or higher, 85 or higher) by year for IDB versus LDB
    For each age group specific chart, IDB should track close to LDB. link

Internal plausibility checks

  1. Chart of death rates by age and sex for each year (based on mltper_1x1.csv/fltper_1x1.csv located in output directory for country!!!)
    Identify strange patterns in death rates for males versus females. link
  2. Chart of death rates by year and sex for each age (based on mltper_1x1.csv/fltper_1x1.csv located in output directory for country!!!)
    Identify strange patterns in death rates for males versus females. link
  3. Chart of mortality deviations compared with adiacent ages. Note that the number of neighboring ages and/or years as well as the p-values for X and the size of R can be modified prior to running the batch program.
    Expect to see large negative deviations at age 1 versus age 0. Patterns of large deviations, e.g., at ages ending in “0” or “5”, may indicate problematic places in the mortality surface. link
  4. Lexis map of total net migration.
    Migration patterns should make sense. Should not show net migration above age 80 (unless there are territorial changes). link

External plausibility checks

  1. Chart of total net migration by calendar year and sex
    Might be used to check that pattern of implied migration is consistent with any available migration data. link
  2. Chart of life expectancy at ages 0, 65, and 80 by sex compared to Sweden
    Might be used to check pattern of life expectancy with Sweden as the benchmark. link

Internal consistency of old vs. new data

  1. Chart of ratio of new vs. old raw and adjusted mortality rates in life table data
    Might be used to locate large discrepancies between old and new data. CS should look at graphs in 16 and 17 to understand whether differences are due to changes in numerators, denominators, or both. link
  2. Chart of ratio of new vs. old death counts in Lexis databse
    Might be used to locate large discrepancies between old and new data. link
  3. Chart of ratio of new vs. old population counts in Lexis database
    Might be used to locate large discrepancies between old and new data. link
  4. Chart of mortality sex ratio surface link
